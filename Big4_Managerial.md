# 💡 Real Example (VERY IMPORTANT)

> “For example, in a microservices-based system, I typically start with a lean team—1 architect, 3–5 developers, 1–2 QA, and shared DevOps.
>
> As the system scales or multiple services are developed in parallel, I expand the team into smaller pods aligned to services or domains.
>
> This ensures scalability without losing ownership or efficiency.”

---

# 🔥 If They Ask: “Can you give numbers?”

## 💡 Strong Answer

> “For a medium-complexity project, I usually start with around 6–10 members.
>
> For larger enterprise systems, it can scale to multiple teams, each owning specific services or modules.”

# 🔥 If They Challenge You

## ❓ “What if timeline is very tight?”

> “In that case, I evaluate whether parallelization is possible.
>
> If yes, I increase team size carefully while ensuring proper coordination.
>
> If not, I align with stakeholders on scope prioritization instead of just adding more people.”

## ❓ “What if team becomes too large?”

> “I split the team into smaller, independent units with clear ownership—often aligned with microservices or business domains—to maintain efficiency.”
