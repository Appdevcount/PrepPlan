**Five tracks to serve:**
1. Upskilling + Certification — career foundation

## The Core Allocation Principle

> **Don't split every day equally. Assign each day a primary identity.**
> The brain context-switches at a cost of 10–15 min per shift. A day with one clear purpose produces 40% more output than a day with four competing modes.

---

## Intellectual Reasoning: Why Each Activity Gets Its Specific Slot

### Why Tuesday = AI Integration (not Monday, not Wednesday)
- Monday: You're re-entering work rhythm after the weekend — best for structured, guided learning (lower decision fatigue, no blank-canvas pressure)
- Tuesday: Mid-week entry point. Mental energy has fully re-engaged, work rhythm is established, but you haven't hit mid-week fatigue yet. This is peak **creative problem-solving energy**
- AI integration work (designing prompts, building pipelines, debugging API calls, experimenting) is open-ended and creative — it demands exactly this cognitive state
- 120 min uninterrupted: AI project work burns ~45 min just loading context and setup. A 90-min session leaves only 45 min of actual building. 120 min gives you a full productive window
- Tuesday also means Wednesday's branding post can reference what you built — natural content pipeline

### Why Saturday = AI Integration (not Sunday)
- Saturday morning: First day off — refreshed, no work-week cognitive residue
- You pick up Tue's AI work 4 days later. That's close enough to still hold mental context (closer than Mon→Fri would be)
- Creative + technical work belongs on Sat. Sunday is wind-down mode — wrong energy for debugging or architecting
- 90 min Sat = you advance the project meaningfully, hit a milestone or a clear stopping point

### Why Sunday = Portfolio Site (not Saturday)
- Sunday is reflective, lower-intensity mode — ideal for the kind of work portfolio needs: writing project descriptions, refining design, organizing content
- You've just had Tue + Sat of AI work — you have fresh material to showcase on the portfolio on Sunday (natural content pipeline: Build → Showcase)
- Portfolio doesn't need creative problem-solving energy; it needs patience and clarity — exactly the Sunday mental state
- Last 10–15 min of Sunday: plan next week's topics (absorbed within the session, no extra time needed)

### Why Friday = Review + Portfolio Sprint (not AI Integration)
- After 4 days of learning, Friday's brain is in consolidation mode — best for reviewing gaps, mock exams, synthesizing knowledge
- A 30-min portfolio sprint at the end is a rewarding creative shift after 90 min of cert review — it's low cognitive load (pushing code, writing one section) and ends the week with a visible artifact
- Friday's 30 min portfolio also ensures weekly progress on the site even if Sunday gets skipped

### Why Mon / Wed / Thu = Branding + Networking output days
- LinkedIn posts published Monday get algorithm amplification through Tuesday — people are checking feeds at week start
- Wednesday is the platform's statistically highest engagement day — your best post goes here
- Thursday networking is warm: people have already seen your Mon + Wed posts — cold outreach becomes warm, your DMs get more replies
- Tue and Fri have zero output overhead — pure learning/building, no mode switches

## The Content Pipeline (How Activities Feed Each Other)

```
Monday: Learn concept (e.g., RAG architecture)
    ↓
Tuesday: Build it → AI integration project uses that concept
    ↓
Wednesday: Brand it → "I built a RAG pipeline — here's what I learned" post
    ↓
Thursday: Network with it → reach out to an AI engineer who commented on your post
    ↓
Friday: Cert it → review the exam Qs that cover RAG + Vector DBs
    ↓
Saturday: Advance it → add a new feature to the AI project
    ↓
Sunday: Showcase it → add the project to portfolio with screenshots and writeup
```

This is not 5 separate tracks. It's one loop — every activity feeds the next.

## Key Principles

- **One day, one identity:** Each day has a primary mode. Don't compromise it for secondary tasks.
- **Tue + Sat are sacred AI blocks:** These are the only sessions long enough for real project flow. Protect them from cert reading or LinkedIn tasks.
- **AI work feeds everything else:** What you build on Tue/Sat becomes your Wed brand post, your Thu networking hook, your Sun portfolio entry, and your Mon learning motivation.
- **Portfolio beats 100 posts:** A live, well-documented portfolio project compresses months of LinkedIn content into a single URL you can share anywhere.
- **The 30-min flex rule:** Fri's 30 min and Sun's last 10 min are your only flexible buffers. Everything else is load-bearing — do not borrow from Mon/Wed/Thu learning blocks.
